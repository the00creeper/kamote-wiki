# ⚖️ The Court Process

The **Kamorte Suprema** is the highest court of justice in Kamote Server and is responsible for resolving disputes, enforcing server rules, and ensuring fairness within the community. Cases may involve player-to-player conflicts such as griefing or theft, or server-wide offenses such as cheating and exploiting.

The judicial process is designed to prioritize:

* Fairness
* Transparency
* Evidence-based rulings
* Equal opportunity for both parties to present their side

***

## Filing a Complaint

All court cases begin with a **complaint ticket**.

A complaint may involve any action that:

* Violates server rules, and
* Causes inconvenience or harm to another player or to the server itself

Initially, only the complainant and staff members can view the complaint ticket. Additional members may be added later if necessary.

***

## Preliminary Investigation

A moderator reviews the complaint and may invite the complainant into a voice channel for further discussion.

Voice communication is **not required**. The important part is that the complainant can:

* Watch the moderator’s stream, and
* Direct the investigation

During the investigation, the moderator may:

* Inspect CoreProtect logs
* Review chat logs
* Check Discord logs
* Search console records
* Review other relevant sources

The moderator will investigate based on the complainant’s requests and directions, but can also independently search for evidence reasonably connected to the complaint while remaining impartial.

The complainant is encouraged to:

* Take screenshots
* Record clips
* Gather evidence

Relevant logs and evidence may identify the perpetrator(s).

***

## Pre-Trial & Discovery Process

Once sufficient evidence is gathered, the complainant submits all evidence into the ticket.

At this stage:

* The complaint becomes an **official court case**
* The ticket becomes a **pre-trial ticket**
* The process of **discovery** begins

The complainant officially becomes the:

* **Prosecutor** — must prove the **guilt** of the defendant with evidence.

The accused party becomes the:

* **Defendant** — must defend their **innocence** with evidence.

The defendant’s in-game rank is temporarily changed to:

* **`ACCUSED`**

{% hint style="warning" %}
Players with the `ACCUSED` rank cannot execute commands until they are acquitted or have served their sentence.
{% endhint %}

### Defendant Rights

Defendants are presumed innocent until proven guilty beyond reasonable doubt. They have the right to:

* Submit evidence defending innocence
* Request moderator assistance in gathering evidence
* Seek legal representation
* Defend themselves during trial

Evidence may include:

* Screenshots
* Videos
* Streams
* CoreProtect logs
* Chat logs
* Console logs
* Discord logs
* Other relevant records

***

## Rules on Evidence

### Verified Evidence Only

{% hint style="warning" %}
Only evidence submitted and verified during pre-trial is valid during trial.
{% endhint %}

Neither party may introduce:

* Surprise evidence
* Unverified logs
* Newly discovered materials during trial proper

If this occurs, the opposing party may object and request the evidence be nullified.

### Purpose of the Pre-Trial Ticket

The pre-trial ticket exists solely for:

* Submitting evidence
* Organizing materials

It is **not** a debate room.

Parties should avoid:

* Arguing
* Defending
* Attacking
* Debating the case inside the ticket

### Evidence Deadline

Evidence submissions close:

* **3 hours before the scheduled trial**

No further evidence may be submitted afterward, even if the trial is rescheduled.

***

## Lawyers & Representation

Players may represent themselves or appoint a lawyer.

A lawyer may be:

* Any server player chosen by the party

Lawyers are optional but may be helpful for:

* Understanding procedures
* Presenting arguments
* Attending in place of unavailable parties

All legal representatives must be identified during pre-trial.

{% hint style="warning" %}
Players may not randomly select someone from the audience during trial to act as their lawyer.
{% endhint %}

***

## Scheduling the Trial

Once discovery is complete:

* Both parties agree on a trial date and time

The case is then publicly announced in the `#court-cases` Discord channel with:

* Case title (e.g., `The00Creeper v. Meepbopmeep et al.`)
* Case code (e.g., `KS-CV-001-2024`)
* Case type (i.e., `Civil Case` or `Criminal Case`)
* Charges filed (e.g., `murder`, `griefing`, etc.)
* Prosecutor(s) (e.g., `The00Creeper`)
* Defendant(s) (e.g., `Meepbopmeep, tayona143, and marktahimik`)
* Date of incident (e.g., `July 19, 2022`)
* Date of trial (e.g., `November 02, 2024`)
* Assigned judge (e.g., `Hon. Jemjem14`)

### Rescheduling

Either party may request rescheduling:

* Up to **1 hour before trial**
* Only for legitimate emergencies

Evidence submissions remain closed based on the **original schedule**.

***

## Types of Cases

### Civil Cases

Player-versus-player conflicts, including:

* Griefing
* Theft
* Killing
* Harassment

Case format:

```
The00Creeper v. Meepbopmeep et al.
```

{% hint style="info" %}
The ending `et al.` is a Latin abbreviation meaning “**and others.”** It is primarily used in legal documents when a text or case has multiple parties, and listing all of them would be too long.
{% endhint %}

### Criminal Cases

Server-versus-player conflicts, including:

* Exploiting
* Cheating
* Abuse of unfair advantages

Case format:

```
People of Kamote v. Meepbopmeep et al.
```

{% hint style="info" %}
In criminal cases the judge also serves as prosecutor
{% endhint %}

***

## Beginning the Trial

Official trials are held at the:

* **Kamorte Suprema**

On Discord, proceedings occur in:

* **“Trial in Session”** voice channel

The court clerk:

* Calls the court to order
* Announces the judge’s entrance

The judge officially declares the court:

* **“In Session”**

***

## Attendance Rules

### Prosecution Absence

If the prosecution fails to appear without notice:

* Trial is rescheduled

If it happens a second time:

* The case is dismissed
* Defendant is acquitted by default

### Defense Absence

If the defense fails to appear without notice:

* Trial may proceed _in absentia_

The defendant risks:

* Losing opportunity to defend themselves
* Default conviction

***

## Swearing Under Oath

Before participating, defendants must swear under oath:

> “I, state your name, solemnly swear to tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth. So help me God.”

{% hint style="warning" %}
Refusing the oath may result in contempt of court, therefore losing the privilege to defend.
{% endhint %}

Multiple defendants are sworn in individually.

***

## Trial by Judge or Jury

Defendants are asked to choose between:

* Trial by Judge — the judge alone determines the verdict; or
* Trial by Jury — five impartial jurors determine the verdict. Jurors must:
  * Be unaffiliated with either party
  * Base decisions solely on presented evidence

{% hint style="info" %}
The first five qualified volunteers become the jury.
{% endhint %}

***

### Jury Oath

The duty of the jury is recited to them:

> “Members of the jury, your duty today is critical. You must remain completely impartial and cast aside all personal bias. You are required to deliver a verdict based solely on the evidence presented in this room, not on speculation or rumor. Pay close attention to both parties, listen to every word, and take note of every important detail. The truth depends on your absolute focus.”

Once they understand their duty, they are sworn in oath:

> “I, state your name, vested with the power of the Kamonstitution, solemnly swear, to review all evidence, with absolute fairness, cast aside personal bias, and deliver a just and truthful verdict, based solely on the law. So help me God.”

{% hint style="info" %}
Jurors are strongly encouraged to take notes.
{% endhint %}

In a trial by judge, the judge must swear the same impartiality oath before acting as sole arbiter.

***

## Trial Proper

### Opening Statements

The prosecution begins by presenting:

* Charges
* Timeline
* Context
* Supporting evidence

The defense then:

* Counters evidence
* Cross-examines arguments
* Provides alternative explanations
* Presents defensive evidence

Both parties may:

* Stream evidence presentation on Discord

{% hint style="warning" %}
Only evidence verified during pre-trial may be used by both parties.
{% endhint %}

### Courtroom Conduct

Arguments should always address the judge (third-person) and not the other party (second-person).

Correct:

> “Your Honor, the evidence shows the defendant stole the diamonds.”

Incorrect:

> “You stole the diamonds.”

The judge may intervene anytime for:

* Clarifications
* Additional context
* Procedural control

### Objections

Parties may object if the opposing side:

* Introduces unverified evidence
* Makes irrelevant arguments
* Attacks parties personally
* Violates courtroom procedure

The judge responds with:

* Objection Sustained — the objection is granted.
* Objection Overruled — the objection is denied.

***

## Jury Deliberation

If jury trial was chosen, the jurors are teleported by the judge to the **Verdict Hall** and are moved to the **“Verdict Room”** voice channel.

Jurors may:

* Discuss evidence
* Debate interpretations
* Attempt to persuade one another

{% hint style="warning" %}
Forcing other jurors to overturn their vote is prohibited.
{% endhint %}

Each juror privately casts either:

* “Guilty”
* “Not Guilty”

### Unanimous Verdict Requirement

Jury verdicts must be unanimous.

* 5/5 jurors vote Guilty = Conviction
* 5/5 jurors vote Not Guilty = Acquittal

If even one juror disagrees:

* The trial becomes a **hung jury**
* A **mistrial** is declared
* A new trial may later be scheduled

***

## Verdict & Sentencing

Once deliberation ends:

* Court resumes session
* Verdict is publicly announced

### If Guilty

Possible punishments include:

* Jail time
* Temporary bans
* Permanent bans
* Additional sanctions

Punishment severity depends on:

* Nature of offense
* Damage caused
* Prior offenses
* Judicial discretion

{% hint style="info" %}
Punishments listed in the [Wiki](../information/rules.md#in-game-offenses-and-sanctions) serve only as general guidelines. The judge may adjust punishments depending on the nature and damage of the crime.
{% endhint %}

Convicted players also receive a **server offense record**.

{% hint style="danger" %}
Repeated convictions may lead to harsher punishments, eventually resulting in bans.
{% endhint %}

### If Not Guilty

The defendant is:

* Acquitted
* Released from charges
* Given no offense record

***

## Adjournment

Once all scheduled cases are completed, the judge formally adjourns the court session.
